Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Mumbai and cluster bus routes.

Mumbai has 272 BEST (Brihanmumbai Electric Supply and Transport) bus routes covering 3,303 stops across the city. Additionally, there are 230 AC bus routes operated by BEST.

Use the following tools:

  • BEST Chalo App: For real-time tracking, schedules, and seat reservations.
  • Google Maps/Moovit App: Provides route details, schedules, and live updates.
  • BEST’s Official Website: For fare information and route maps.

S145 is a premium AC route connecting Konkan Bhavan, Belapur (Navi Mumbai) to World Trade Centre, Cuffe Parade (South Mumbai) via Atal Setu. Key stops include Sai Sangam, Ulwe node, CSMT, Churchgate, and Eastern Freeway. It operates twice daily in the morning (Belapur to WTC) and evening (WTC to Belapur).

The longest route is 526LTD, spanning 36 km with 82 stops from Khodadad Circle (Dadar) to Kharghar Jalvayu Vihar.

BEST (Brihanmumbai Electric Supply and Transport) is Mumbai’s primary public bus operator, established in 1873. It operates 2,700+ buses (including AC and non-AC) across 272 routes, serving 4 million+ daily passengers.

The largest bus terminal in India is ISBT (Inter-State Bus Terminal) in Delhi. In Mumbai, major bus hubs include Dadar, Andheri, and Powai’s Forest Street Bus Stop (noted for 24/7 service).
